Marcus Aurelius

A king, a philosopher, an intellectual disciple

Marcus Aurelius Life Timeline:

  • Marcus Aurelius was born on April 26, 121 known as a dedicated student on philosophy that emphasized fate, reason and self-restraint.
  • In 138, Marcus Aurelius was adopted by Antoninus (who would be known as Emperor Pius Antonius) because his serious and hard-working nature was noticed
  • In 140, Aurelius became a consul, or leader of the senate for Emperor Antonius
  • As years past, he received more responsibilities and official powers
  • In 145, he married Faustina, the emperor’s daughter
  • In 161, his adoptive father passed away, and Aurelius rose to power and was officially then known as Emperor Marcus Aurelius
  • In 160s, he went to his first battle with the Parthian empire for control over lands in the East, alongside his generals, especially Avidius Cassius and his brother Verus
  • In late 160s, he face another military conflict with German tribes, as they crossed the Danube River and attacked a Roman city. The battle was victorious, but he lost his brother Varus in battle
  • In 175, his best general, Avidus Cassius challenged Aurelius' throne to be the next Roman Emperor.Aurelius then travel to the East to regain control.
  • Although the battle is again victorious, his wife passed away during his trip to the East. Together they had many children, though some did not live for long.
  • In 177, Aurelius made his son Commodus his co-ruler while battling the German Tribes once again
  • On march 17th 180, Aurelius passed away at the age of 58. His son Commodus became emperor and soon ended the northern military efforts

He ruled in a time of a debilitating plague, buried eight of his children, lost his brother in battle, spent a decade at war, betrayed by his most trusted general, and lost his wife in the process. Yet his character never wavered. He was acutely aware of his own weaknesses and shortcomings, and was committed to becoming a better person throughout his life.

" The most essential thing in life is to realize there are things we can control, and things we cannot. The only things in our control are our own thoughts, opinions, and choices. Everything else — our fate, possessions, reputation, other people’s behavior, and environmental events — are out of our control. We have no power over them. But crucially, it’s our choice how we think about them. "

- Marcus Aurelius
